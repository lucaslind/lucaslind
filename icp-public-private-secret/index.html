International Center of Photography / Public, Private, Secret exhibition
2016

Design of the ICP’s new space on the Bowery and the inaugural exhibition, Public, Private, Secret. The exhibition examines the concept of privacy in today’s society and studies how contemporary self-identity is tied to public visibility. The design investigates photography’s role in breaking and resetting the boundaries of social and personal privacy.

Exhibition design for common room. Project team: John Buonnocore, Geoff Han, Maria Ibañez, and Todd Rouhe. Curated by Charlotte Cotton.
