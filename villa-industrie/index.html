Villa Industrie
2013

“The Forest of Bourg-Marie is the darkest, the deepest, the most impenetrable, the most forbidding. The stars of spring that light up other woods seem here rarely to pierce through the cold, hard ground to the sun: the sun itself seldom penetrates the thick branches of fir and pine and hemlock. The tints of autumn that beautify the death of the year in other places are absent from its partially-cleared fringe of pine-tasseled ground; there seems no color, no motion, no warmth anywhere.”
— S. Frances Harrison, 1898

A proposal for an industrial loft building in the Bronx based on three spaces of production: early 20th century Albert Kahn manufacturing buildings, the Culture Shed, and the co-working spaces of today’s corporate “avant-garde." The forest of structural folumns and exploded cores attempt to defy the gridded and indeterminate nature of space in the shed typology. As the only division of space, the columnar field allows corner offices, individual cubicles, couches and pool tables to co-exist in a single space.

Professors: David Wallance and Jay Hibbs.
