Lost Horizon
2012

This project attempts to rethink the thick 2d of the mat as a tool to control perception and visibility. Mat-building is notable for its shallow, dense section, horizontal extension and connectivity. Here, it is organized on a new type of grid in the already grid-abundant Venice Beach, CA, a new patch based around aggregation of a primitive unit. The undulating roof alludes to the effect on a boat’s observer on a turbulent sea but co-opts this effect through projection onto the city skyline, surrounding mountains and horizon line of the Pacific Ocean.

The roof indicates the interior conditions. The lowest areas imply single-story retail space. As the roof rises, it houses first sprawling two-story residential and retail space, then more condensed residential units with lofts that peer out of the sloped skylights, and finally narrow three story units with balconies that look out across the roof. The directionality of views out from the highest points ensures privacy. Large courtyards puncture through to the ground at the highest points of the roofs, and smaller courtyards occur elsewhere. Thus, vertical growth coincides with subtraction in plan.

Professor: Roger Sherman.
