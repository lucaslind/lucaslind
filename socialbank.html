Social Bank
2013

The Fulton Mall site in downtown Brooklyn is situated at a significant nexus of cultures, demographics and income levels as well as a confluence of zoning typologies, urban grids and axes. The social network bank is understood as a place where these social intersections are articulated and where interactions and encounters become a medium of exchange and a type of currency. As an alternative to the traditional and often under-utilized horizontal datum of the public park, the bar bank proposes introducing a new public condition in the area in the form of a three-dimensional all-terrain network of social space.

Professor: Lindy Roy.
