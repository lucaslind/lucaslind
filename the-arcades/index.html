The Arcades: Contemporary Art and Walter Benjamin
2017

An exhibition that explores The Arcades Project through works of contemporary art representing the subjects of each of the book’s thirty-six chapters. The exhibition combines archival material from the Benjamin archive in Berlin, architectural models, and artwork to evoke the elaborate structure of Benjamin’s text. Transposing Benjamin’s arcades to the galleries of the Jewish Museum, the exhibition invites the visitor to take on the role of the flâneur.

Participating Artists: Erica Baum, Walead Beshty, Milena Bonilla, Andrea Bowers, Nicholas Buffon, Chris Burden, Haris Epaminonda and Daniel Gustav Cramer, Simon Evans, Walker Evans, Claire Fontaine, Lee Friedlander, Rodney Graham, Andreas Gursky, Raymond Hains, Pierre Huyghe, Voluspa Jarpa, Jesper Just, Sanya Kantarovsky, Mike Kelley, Tim Lee, Jorge Macchi, Adam Pendleton, Martín Ramírez, Bill Rauhauser, Mary Reid Kelley, Ry Rocklen, Markus Schinwald, Collier Schorr, Cindy Sherman, Taryn Simon, Joel Sternfeld, Mungo Thomson, Timm Ulrichs, Guido van der Werve, James Welling, and Cerith Wyn Evans
