Primitive Pinball
2012

Like architecture, the pinball machine originated in nature. Its forms and mechanics were modeled after life forms and biological systems. The original pinball machine had four elements: the launcher (birth), the flipper (will), the path (existential struggle), and the hole (death).

The antithesis to the electronic Fordist–Spectacle pinball machine, primitive pinball creates a false origins story of the pinball machine and serves as an investigation into the human condition. Primitive pinballâ€™s simple mechanics, confusing tubes and repetitive but difficult gameplay provide endless infatuation despite the essential pointlessness of playing.

Ironically, the form disguises the hightech fabrication techniques involved in making the ostensibly low-tech primitive pinball machine.

29 in. x 20 in. x 6 in. Laminated CNC-milled plywood, surgical tubing, vacuum-formed styrene and PETG.
